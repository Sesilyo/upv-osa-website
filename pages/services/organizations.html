STUDENT ORGANIZATION & ACTIVITIES

Providing opportunities for development outside the classroom are the different student organizations and activities on campus. Coordinated by the OSA, these activities serve as venues for development of personal talents and abilities as well as leadership skills and responsibilities. Students are also exposed to various training programs such as leadership seminars and psychosocial activities. SOA also allows the students to organize and facilitate their own activities and events, which they deem relevant or integral to their academic experience in the University.

Services & Programs

Recognition of Student Organizations

Student organizations apply for or renew their recognition every Academic Year to operate legitimately. Submitted applications are assessed and student activities conducted in the previous academic year are evaluated to determine their recognition status as fully recognized or probationary.

Leadership Development

SOA provides leadership training to recognized student organizations and also equips members of UPV DUCES, the volunteer arm of the Office of Student Affairs. Different resource persons and facilitators train the student leaders in the University. In return, UPV DUCES conduct a series of leadership training sessions to House Council officers of the different residential units of the University.

Facilitation/Monitoring of Student Organization Activities

All student organizations are required to file application forms and submit necessary requirements for every activity being conducted. The SOA unit screens and monitors the different activities of the students and various student organizations recognized under OSA.

Student organizations applying for initaition rites must file a written application through the OSA. This is in accordance with RA 11053 (Anti-Hazing Act of 2018) to monitor their process of recruiting new members without inflicting harm or injury.

The SOA Unit also approves posting of publicity materials of students and student organizations as well as facilitates various requests of students i.e. off-campus activities, financial assistance, alternative or excuse from classes, etc.

SOA Orientation

A semestral orientation is conducted for all student organizations. This is to inform existing organizations, both college-based and university-wide, as well as faculty advisers, about the policies and guidelines governing student organizations including the Anti-Hazing Law.

Types of Organizations

University Organizations

These are student organizations whose members belong to two or more colleges of the university.

College Organizations

These are student organizations whose members belong to exclusively one college.

Based on the nature of their activities, student organizations may be classified as:

  • College Student Councils
  • Publications (e.g. "Pagbutlak", "Kasanag", "Accounts", "Salambaw")
  • Interest Groups
  • Fraterneties and Sororities
  • Chapters/Branches of Recognized National or Regional Organizations
  • Cultural Performing Groups of the University
  • Academic Based Organization

Rights & Privileges of Recognized Student Organizations

  • Use of campus facilities (grounds, classrooms, buildings, et cetera) and personnel devices, subject to rules amd regulations on the use of such, and provided official academic activities are not prejudiced.

  • Representation, through the UPV Student Council, in standing committees which concern student welfare and interest.

  • Officers and/or members of fully recognized student organizations may be appointed representatives of the University in community activities as requested or reccomended by the Chancellor or his/her representatives.
